What crane does

01

Capture without breaking flow

Press ⌘⇧Space over any app and a quiet bar floats in, already focused. Type the thought, press ↵, and it's gone — you never left the thing you were doing. That's the whole app: nothing to file, nothing to close.

02

Thoughts or links, one bar

Pasting a URL? Hit ⌘L and the bar flips to link mode. Thoughts and links land in the same history, searchable with ⌘F — one place for everything your brain throws at you mid-task.

03

No account. No server. No network.

crane makes zero network requests — no API key, no sign-in, nothing to phone home. Every note lives in a single file on your Mac. Reset it all from the menu whenever you like.

04

Native and nearly invisible

It's a menu-bar app, not a workspace. No dock icon, no big window — just a floating bar when you need it and a small popover for recent entries, tags and stats when you don't.

On-device intelligence

Tags that file themselves.

crane reads each thought and tags it with a topic or two, so you can find things later without having remembered to file them. That runs entirely on your Mac, through Apple Intelligence's Foundation Models — your thoughts are never sent anywhere.

Tagging happens after the save. It never slows down capture.
